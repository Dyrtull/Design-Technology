Cambridge Design & Technology

Comprehensive Comparison: IGCSE 0445 vs AS Level 9705

Program Overview

IGCSE Design & Technology 0445

Foundation Level (Ages 14-16)

Introduces core design and manufacturing principles

  • Common content + 1 specialist option
  • Practical project-based learning
  • Focus on making and evaluation
  • Workshop safety and basic processes

AS Level Design & Technology 9705

Advanced Level (Ages 16-18)

In-depth design theory and advanced applications

  • Comprehensive design theory
  • Advanced material science
  • Design movements and principles
  • Emerging technologies
IGCSE Duration 2 years typical
AS Duration 1 year typical
IGCSE Prerequisites Basic secondary education
AS Prerequisites IGCSE or equivalent

IGCSE Design & Technology 0445

Common Content - Product Design

  • Design Process: Need identification, brief development, research, idea generation
  • Implementation: Material preparation, accurate methods, process selection
  • Health & Safety: Workshop safety, tool usage, safety symbols
  • Communication: Technical drawing, sketching, CAD awareness
  • Sustainability: Energy sources, recycling, material selection
  • Control Systems: Input, processing, output, feedback

Specialist Options (Choose 1)

Resistant Materials

  • Plastics, Woods, Metals
  • Smart materials (thermochromic, SMA)
  • Shaping, joining, finishing processes
  • Composites (Kevlar, CFRP, GRP)

Systems & Control

  • Structures, Mechanisms, Electronics
  • Levers, gears, motion transmission
  • Circuit building, components
  • Logic gates, control systems

Graphic Products

  • Technical drawing, orthographic projection
  • Perspective, developments (nets)
  • Layout, presentation techniques
  • ICT applications, CAD/CAM awareness
Key Focus: Practical making skills combined with design thinking and evaluation

AS Level Design & Technology 9705

Core Topics

1. Design Process Iterative & intuitive design, design thinking stages
2. Design Principles Good design concepts, design movements
3. Communication Advanced drawing techniques, digital tools
4. Society Impact Inclusive design, cultural considerations
5. Sustainability Environmental impact, sustainable practices
6. Health & Safety Risk assessment, safe practices

Advanced Material Science

  • Traditional: Papers, woods, metals, polymers
  • Composites: Foam core, GRP, CFRP, manufactured boards
  • Smart Materials: Thermochromic, SMA, hydrogels, shape memory polymers
  • Modern Materials: Nanomaterials, metal foams, bioplastics
  • Biodegradable: PLA, PHB, corn/potato starch plastics

Emerging Technologies

Manufacturing

  • 3D printing & rapid prototyping
  • CAD/CAM integration
  • Robotics in manufacturing
  • AI applications

Design Tools

  • Virtual Reality (VR)
  • Advanced CAD systems
  • Simulation software
  • Digital collaboration
Key Focus: Theoretical understanding of design principles with advanced material science and emerging technologies

AS Level 9705 Assessment

Paper 1: Written Paper

Duration: 2 hours
Marks: 80 marks
Weighting: 40%

Tests topics 1-12 through structured questions requiring extended responses.

Paper 3: Design Communication

Duration: 2 hours
Marks: 60 marks
Weighting: 30%

Drawing and communication skills including orthographic, isometric, and perspective drawings.

Component 2: Coursework Project

Duration: Portfolio work
Marks: 60 marks
Weighting: 30%

Design development project focusing on analysis, development, and evaluation.

Teacher Training Requirements

IGCSE 0445 Teacher Preparation

Essential Qualifications

  • Design & Technology degree or equivalent technical qualification
  • Teaching qualification (PGCE or equivalent)
  • Workshop safety certification
  • Experience in chosen specialist area

Practical Skills Required

  • Workshop Management: Safety protocols, tool maintenance
  • Material Knowledge: Properties, processes, applications
  • Assessment Skills: Project marking, moderation
  • CAD/CAM: Basic computer-aided design skills

AS Level 9705 Teacher Preparation

Advanced Qualifications

  • Degree in Design, Engineering, or related field
  • Industry experience preferred
  • Knowledge of design history and movements
  • Research and analysis skills

Theoretical Expertise Required

  • Design Theory: Principles, movements, analysis
  • Material Science: Advanced properties, emerging materials
  • Technology Trends: AI, VR, 3D printing, Industry 4.0
  • Sustainability: Environmental impact, circular design

Professional Development Pathways

Assessment Training Moderation, standardization workshops
